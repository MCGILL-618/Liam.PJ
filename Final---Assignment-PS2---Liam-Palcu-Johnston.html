Question Five - Part D - Intepretating Graphs from Part B and C

My friend is wrong. As the size of samples and the number of simulations increase the distribution looks more and more normal. This supports the central limit theorem. This theorem posits that as one takes more samples from a population the distribution will become normal. In other words, it is irrelevant whether a samples distribution is normal or not, if one takes repeated samples the eventual distribution will become normal.
